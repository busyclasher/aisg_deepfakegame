PHASE 1
DEEPFAKE TIPS
🔍
Check the Video. Look for signs of AI-generated media — unnatural blinking, facial warping, or lip-sync mismatch.
📋
Evaluate the Content. What inconsistencies or unusual patterns can you spot? Does the background match? Do shadows make sense?
🌐
Research the Source. Where does the video come from? Is it shared by a credible account? When was it first published?
⚡
Evaluate Before You Share. Even if something seems shocking or true, pause before spreading it. Deepfakes rely on emotional reactions.
🤖 Common AI Tell-Signs:
Unnatural eye movement · Too-smooth skin · Weird ear/hair edges · Audio-lip mismatch · Inconsistent lighting on face · Blurry teeth · Background objects that "swim"
PHASE 2
IMAGE ANALYSIS TIPS
🔍
Scan the Edges. AI edits often blur or smear at the boundary between the manipulated area and the original — look for soft, melted edges that don't match the surrounding sharpness.
👁️
Check Facial Features. Teeth, ears, and eyes are the hardest for AI to render cleanly. Look for blurry tooth gaps, ear edges that blend into the background, or misaligned eyelids.
✋
Inspect Hands. AI-generated hands frequently have the wrong number of fingers, unnatural joint angles, or anatomy that loses structure on close inspection.
🌿
Look at Textures. Fur, hair, and fine detail are often smeared together in manipulated regions. Real textures have consistent, repeating structure — AI textures lose that at the edges.
💡
Compare Lighting. Does the light direction on the edited region match the rest of the image? AI composites often have subtle mismatches in shadow angle or highlight intensity.

S.U.R.E FRAMEWORK
Welcome to Deepfake Decoder, a media literacy game where you learn to spot REAL vs FAKE media. Before starting the quiz, use the S.U.R.E. framework:
S
Source — Where did the video come from? Is it from a reliable account or platform?
U
Understand — Look carefully at the face, lighting, lip movement, and background. Do they look natural?
R
Research — Check whether trusted news outlets or official sources report the same thing.
E
